Overview

What is the Agriculture Editor and Location Service?

The Agriculture Editor and Location Service is a comprehensive open desktop solution for documenting, managing and visualizing geographic livestock herd distributions, individual animal locations, and animal movements within and between premises locations.

The Agriculture Editor and Location Service is a suite of tools that integrates state of the art Geographic Information System GIS technologies based on the OpenGIS Key Hole Markup LanguageKML with Tracker Premises livestock management information.

Project Goals

As livestock markets continue to adapt to customer requirements, more livestock markets will require animal source information to do business. Yet even for producers not facing this requirement, just knowing where animals are located while still on the premises is considered must have requirement.

Making livestock location information easy to document, lookup and easy to electronically inter-exchange is the primary goal of the Agriculture Editor and Location Service project.

The Agriculture Editor and Location Service provide models, editors and services for the creation and management of documents that:

  • Reference livestock premises to geographic locations

  • Create associated groups of business related livestock premises within geographic regions

  • Create named geographical sub-locations within single livestock premises

  • Locate Animals that have moved to geographical sub-locations within a premises

  • Locate Animals that have moved to another premises within associated groups of business related livestock premises

  • Assign animal positions based on visual sighting records or data inputs from industry standard GPS devices

  • Will serve as the foundation for a full featured distributed livestock Traceability service

Functional Overview

The Agriculture Editor is used to create documents that assign Geographical Locations to Livestock premises.

Note

The Agriculture Editor is dependent on two other Tracker Desktop Features, the Premises Editor and the Geographic Editor. Agriculture documents created by the Agriculture Editor reference elements contained in documents created by these two editors. Geography attributes in the Agriculture documents are references to Container elements in the *.kml documents created by the Geographic Editor. Livestock attributes in Agriculture documents are referenced to Premises elements in the *.premises documents created by Premises Editors.

The Location Service provides name and geolocation resolution based on the information contained in specially designated Agriculture documents. The Location Service enhances all features containing location derived attributes.

The Agriculture Editor and Location Service Feature adds geographical visualization, location resolution and geographic analytical utilities to the Tracker Desktop Business Intelligence Toolkit:

  • Simple end user visualization of premises geographies through integration with industry standard Geobrowsers.

  • Geolocation features benefit all types of producers. For those using electronic automation with GPS and RFID readers and those using manual key entry of animal history events

  • Desktop editors, views, and utilities for modeling agriculture associations allowing livestock producers auditors, and processors to geographically -

    • manage internal, inter-association and intra-association animal movements and information flows

    • create marketing and/or auditing reports for regional and local based farm products

  • Easily view the location of animals within the local premises.

    • Resolution of locations based on animal Position events that were input from GPS devices associated with RFID readers. Where Position coordinates determine the location names based on best fit (smallest area) of list of geographically specified locations

    • Visual sighting of animals with manual assignment of location names selected from list of geographically specified locations

    • Most current Sighting event or Position event determines location

  • Easily view the destination of animals moved to other business partners
